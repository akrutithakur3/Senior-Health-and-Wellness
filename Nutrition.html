Nutrition

Eating a balanced and nutritious diet is important for people of all ages, but it's especially important for seniors. As we age, our bodies require fewer calories, but more of certain nutrients. Here are some tips for eating well as you age:

Nutrition Tips

  • Eat a variety of fruits and vegetables, including dark, leafy greens and bright-colored fruits and veggies.
  • Choose whole grains, like brown rice and quinoa, over refined grains, like white bread and pasta.
  • Include lean protein sources, like chicken and fish, in your meals.
  • Limit your intake of processed and packaged foods, which can be high in sodium and unhealthy fats.
  • Stay hydrated by drinking plenty of water and limiting your intake of sugary drinks like soda and juice.

For more information on nutrition for seniors, consult a registered dietitian or your healthcare provider.
